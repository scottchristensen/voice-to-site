# SpeakYour.site Brand Guidelines

## Brand Overview

SpeakYour.site is a voice-to-website platform built for small to medium-sized businesses and non-technical professionals who want to get online quickly without complexity.

**Target Audience:** Small business owners, tradespeople, home service professionals (HVAC, plumbers, roofers, cleaners, electricians, landscapers), and B2C businesses who may not be tech-enabled.

**Core Message:** Get online in minutes, not days.

**Brand Promise:** Empowering hardworking professionals to establish a legitimate web presence and grow their business through the simplest possible interface—their voice.

---

## Brand Voice & Tone

**Voice:** Confident & straightforward

**How we sound:**
- Direct and no-nonsense, like the tradespeople we serve
- Plain-spoken, never using tech jargon or buzzwords
- Respectful of their time and expertise
- Action-oriented and results-focused

**Example phrases:**
- "Speak your business into existence"
- "Your voice. Your website. Your success."
- "No typing. No tech skills. No problem."
- "Built in minutes. Lasts forever."

---

## Messaging Focus Areas

Every piece of copy should ladder up to one of these core benefits:

1. **Getting online** - Fast, simple web presence
2. **Getting more business** - Attract new customers
3. **Highlighting your work** - Showcase expertise and craftsmanship
4. **Getting more clients** - Grow customer base
5. **Making more money** - Increase revenue
6. **Being more successful** - Business growth and expansion
7. **Being legitimized** - Professional credibility and trust

## Visual Identity

### Color Palette

**Primary: Light Sage Green (Variable Intensity)**
- Very light sage: `#EBF2E8` or `#F0F5ED` (barely-there, airy)
- Soft sage: `#D8E8D4` or `#DCE9D8` (visible but gentle)
- Medium-light sage: `#C9DEC4` or `#CFDEC9` (more pronounced)
- Usage: Alternating section backgrounds with varying intensities for visual interest
- Conveys: Fresh, natural, optimistic, light and easy

**Secondary: Cream/White**
- Cream: `#FAF9F6` or `#F5F5DC`
- White: `#FFFFFF`
- Usage: Main backgrounds, cards, breathing room, alternating with sage sections
- Conveys: Clarity, simplicity, cleanliness, spaciousness

**Accent: Warm Terracotta/Burnt Orange**
- Hex: `#D4764A` or `#C7754C`
- Usage: CTAs, key actions, important highlights, links
- Conveys: Energy, action, warmth, approachability, optimism

**Text Colors:**
- Rich brown/espresso: `#4A3525` or `#3D2F1F` for headlines and emphasis
- Charcoal gray: `#2D2D2D` or `#3A3A3A` for body text
- White `#FFFFFF` on terracotta buttons
- Terracotta `#D4764A` for interactive elements and links

### Typography

**Font Family:** Sturdy, grounded sans-serif
- Recommended: Work Sans, Space Grotesk, or similar
- Fallback: system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif

**Type Scale:**
- Headlines: Bold (700), large and impactful
- Body copy: Regular (400) or Medium (500)
- CTAs: Semi-bold (600) or Bold (700)

**Headline Style:**
- Short, punchy statements (3-8 words)
- Direct and benefit-focused
- No em-dashes in copy
- Examples:
  - "Speak. Build. Launch."
  - "Your voice builds your website"
  - "Get online in minutes"
  - "More clients. More money."

**Copy Length:**
- Very minimal throughout
- Headline + 1 short sentence maximum per section
- Respect the user's time
- Let imagery do the talking

## Logo & Wordmark

**Primary Logo:** SpeakYour.site
- Text-based wordmark with simple icon/symbol
- Icon options: Microphone, speech waveform, or abstract voice mark
- Sturdy sans-serif typography
- Can emphasize ".site" as design element

**Logo Usage:**
- Maintain generous whitespace around logo
- Use on cream/white backgrounds primarily
- Dark version (rich brown) on light sage backgrounds

## Imagery Guidelines

### Photography Style

**Subject Matter:**
- Real tradespeople at work
- Authentic job sites and work environments
- Tools, hands, craft details
- Diverse representation across trades, ages, genders, backgrounds

**Aesthetic:**
- Natural lighting, not overly staged
- Authentic settings, real work environments
- Environmental portraits showing people in their element
- Pride, professionalism, and craftsmanship

**What to Avoid:**
- NO stock photo clichés (forced smiles, overly polished corporate imagery)
- NO sterile studio settings
- NO generic "tech startup" aesthetic
- Avoid overly busy or cluttered compositions

### Image Treatment

- Clean, high-quality images
- Subtle overlays allowed when needed for text legibility
- Generous negative space within images when possible
- Images should feel grounded and authentic, never artificial
- Bright, naturally-lit photos that feel optimistic and energetic

---

## Layout & Spacing

### Principles

**Generous Whitespace:**
- Breathing room between all elements
- Content doesn't fight for attention
- Clean lines and uncluttered layouts
- Premium feel through simplicity

**Section Structure:**
- Alternate between cream/white and light sage green backgrounds (varying sage intensities)
- Each section focused on ONE key message
- Minimal copy: headline + 1 sentence
- Clear visual hierarchy
- Light, airy feel throughout

**Grid & Alignment:**
- Clean, simple grid systems
- Left-aligned or center-aligned based on section
- Consistent padding and margins
- No busy patterns or gradients

### Mobile-First Considerations

**CTAs on Mobile:**
- Full-width buttons
- Easy thumb targets (minimum 44px height)
- Generous tap areas
- Terracotta accent color for maximum visibility

**Responsive Spacing:**
- Maintain generous whitespace even on small screens
- Stack elements cleanly
- Readable font sizes (minimum 16px body copy)
- Touch-friendly navigation

---

## Component Guidelines

### Call-to-Action Buttons

**Primary CTAs (most important actions):**
- Background: Terracotta `#D4764A`
- Text: White or cream
- Style: Solid, bold, high contrast
- Mobile: Full width
- Desktop: Generous padding, prominent but not overwhelming

**Secondary CTAs (less critical actions):**
- Outline style in terracotta or white
- Subtle hover states
- Still clear and clickable

**Button Copy:**
- Action-oriented verbs
- Short and direct (1-3 words)
- Examples: "Get Started", "Try It Now", "Start Building", "Speak Your Site"

### Navigation

**Header/Nav:**
- Brand name: SpeakYour.site
- Minimal navigation items (3-5 max)
- Clear, direct labels
- Mobile: Hamburger menu if needed, but prefer simple inline nav

### Forms

- Minimal input fields
- Clear labels
- Generous padding
- Error states in terracotta
- Success states can use subtle green

---

## Brand Dos and Don'ts

### ✅ DO

- Use plain, straightforward language
- Show real tradespeople and authentic work
- Maintain generous whitespace
- Keep it simple and scannable
- Focus on tangible business outcomes (more clients, more money, legitimacy)
- Use solid colors and clean lines
- Respect users' time with minimal copy
- Show diversity in imagery

### ❌ DON'T

- NO tech jargon or buzzwords
- NO stock photo clichés
- NO busy patterns or gradients
- NO purple gradients (ever)
- NO em-dashes in copy
- NO overly long copy blocks
- NO fake or staged imagery
- NO complicated navigation or layouts
- NO tiny text or cramped spacing

## Implementation Notes

This brand system is designed to:
- Stand out from sterile tech platforms
- Feel approachable to non-tech users
- Convey professionalism and legitimacy
- Be simple to implement and maintain
- Scale across all touchpoints (web, email, marketing)

The combination of light sage green, cream, and terracotta creates a fresh, optimistic palette that feels approachable, energetic, and light. The varying sage intensities add visual interest while maintaining simplicity. The minimal copy and generous spacing respect users' time and attention. The imagery of real small business owners and tradespeople creates authenticity and relatability.

**Remember:** Less is more. Every element should earn its place on the page.
